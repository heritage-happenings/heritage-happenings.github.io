### 2. Techniques for Creating Patterns and Shapes

* **Cane Creation**
Â  Â  - A "cane" is a log of clay with a pattern running through its entire length. It is made by combining and manipulating different colors of clay.
* **Technique 1: Skinner Blend Cane**
Â  Â  - **Color Blending:** To create a gradient, different colored pieces of clay are laid out to overlap. This layered clay is repeatedly folded and fed through the pasta roller about 17 times to achieve a smooth "Skinner blend." The clay should always be fed into the roller with the fold side down to push out air.
Â  Â  - **Patterned Sheet:** The resulting rectangular sheet with a color gradient is cut, stacked, and rolled through the pasta machine again to create a long, thin strip.
Â  Â  - **Constructing the Cane:** The strip is rolled into a cylinder, which is then cut into quarters. The pieces are reshaped and reassembled to form a triangular prism with an internal pattern. This process can be repeated with other colors to create multiple triangular canes.
Â  Â  - **Assembling the Cane:** Two different colored triangular canes are placed together (one reversed) and joined. The combined piece is cut in half, flipped, and rejoined to create a symmetrical, butterfly-like pattern. This final assembled block is the "cane."
